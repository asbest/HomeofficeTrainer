Idea in 30 seconds

A regular bicycle + trainer + generator = power for your desk. Many people can continuously pedal 60–100 W. With a buffer battery and suitable electronics, this can power a laptop (≈50 W) and a monitor (≈25 W) – at least temporarily or in mixed operation with battery + pedaling.

Note: Net output depends heavily on person, ergonomics, and electronics. Plan with 50–70% overall efficiency from pedals to usable electricity.

Variant A – Budget & Simple

Core: 24 V DC motor as generator (e.g. from scooter/treadmill), 12 V LiFePO4 battery as buffer, USB-C PD car charger for laptop. Optional small pure sine inverter (150–300 W) for 230 V.

Upcycling possible Little electronics

Variant B – Comfort & Feature-rich

Core: E-bike hub motor as 3-phase generator + rectifier + MPPT charge controller (12/24 V) for optimal yield. LiFePO4 battery + pure sine inverter 300–600 W for mains devices.

Larger buffer MPPT for better efficiency

Variant C – Grid feed-in (with professional)

Only with certified micro-inverter (e.g. for balcony PV) and professional installer: Generator → DC conversion to PV input window → micro-inverter → socket. Approved anti-islanding & standards required.

Warning: Grid-parallel operation requires approval & compliance. Always follow VDE-AR-N 4105 & local regulations and involve a licensed electrician.

Ergonomics & Comfort Add-ons

  • Trainer (rear wheel) or direct drive – stable, quiet
  • Adjust flywheel/resistance (gear choice vs. load)
  • USB-C PD 100 W, 12 V sockets, LED light, fan
  • Display (voltage/current/power), cadence sensor

Recommended concrete products (examples, second-hand possible):
  • Generator: PM DC motor MY1016, 24 V / 300 W (scooter class, brushed DC).
  • Trainer: Wheel-on trainer e.g. Tacx Blue Matic T2650 (or comparable Elite/Minoura).
  • Drive: Gates PowerGrip HTD-5M timing belt (15 mm) + 2 pulleys (e.g. 20T motor Ø 10 mm; 50–60T trainer roller, depending on shaft).
  • Electronics (later): Simple DC-DC regulation to 13.8–14.4 V if battery planned.
Note: For the very first test, motor + belt + simple mount are enough. Battery/electronics can be added later.
Sustainable shopping: Many parts can be sourced second-hand (motor, trainer, battery case). Prefer LiFePO4 over lead-acid – more durable & eco-friendly.
